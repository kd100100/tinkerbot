Congratulations on getting selected for the final round.

The task for today is: “To control RPM of DC motor using Analog Sensors”.

Judging will be primarily based on the time taken to complete the task. The maximum time allotted is 45 minutes. Even if you fail to complete the task, marks will be awarded based on your partially completed circuit and code. You are given 10 mins to read through these instructions and to ask doubts if you have any. Participants should share screen throughout the round.

If you complete your task and show the output within time, full marks will be awarded for code, circuit, and simulation. Further, the winner will be judged based on the time taken.
If you face any technical issues in connecting to the meet, contact the coordinators through the WhatsApp group.

Task Details
You can only use any one of the following sensors:
- Photoresistor
- TMP36 Temperature sensor
- UltraSonic sensor
- Flex Sensor
- Force Sensor
- Gas Sensor

The main task is to control a DC motor according to the analog value from the sensor.
The RPM for your motor should never drop to 0.
Say for example, if the analog value from the Sensor increases then the speed of the DC motor should also increase proportionally and vice - versa.

Hints
- You can just use 2 motors, that’s enough.
- Motor driver is not necessary (remember task 1).
- Choose the sensor wisely!
